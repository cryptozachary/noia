1. Topic

Practical business / monetary use cases for an OpenClaw-style autonomous computer-use agent.

2. Executive Summary

[Inferred] The strongest near-term business case is not selling a general “autonomous employee.” It is selling a bounded workflow with clear success criteria, auditability, and human approval where needed.

[Inferred] The discussion converged on a sequenced commercialization path:

- First: **Web QA regression and synthetic transaction monitoring**
  - Best as a low-liability proof wedge.
  - Useful for demonstrating durability, observability, and basic economics.

- Second: **Approval-gated AP / vendor-portal operations**
  - Strongest likely near-term monetization after reliability is proven.
  - Examples: invoice status checks, portal updates, exception prep, document-to-portal workflows.

- Third: **Structured legacy-UI exception recovery**
  - Strongest longer-term moat versus APIs and traditional RPA.
  - Only credible if limited to narrow, repeatable exception classes.

[Established from discussion consensus] The key gating metric is not demo quality or raw task completion. It is **cost per successful task after human oversight**, including rework,
